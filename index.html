How to play Rummy 500 🎓

  1. The goal: be the first to reach 500 points. Points come from cards you play onto the table; cards stuck in your hand at the end of a round count against you.
  2. Your turn, step 1 — Draw: take the top card of the deck, or take from the discard pile. You can grab the top discard for free, or dig deeper — but then you take every card on top of it too, and you must play the card you dug for immediately in a meld. Grabbed too much by mistake? The ↩️ Put back button returns everything to the pile, as long as you haven't played a card yet.
  3. Step 2 — Meld (optional): lay down a set (3–4 cards of the same rank, like 7♠7♥7♦) or a run (3+ cards of the same suit in a row, like 4♣5♣6♣). You can also lay off cards onto any meld on the table — even your opponent's — and those points are yours!
  4. Step 3 — Discard: end your turn by tossing one card onto the discard pile. Careful what you toss — see the next rule.
  5. Call RUMMY! 🚨: if a discard could have been played on a meld already on the table, the other player can call RUMMY before drawing. The card jumps into that meld with the points going to the caller — and as a penalty, the caller takes one card, sight unseen, from the careless discarder's hand. The bots watch for this too, so mind your discards.
  6. Card points: 2 through 9 = 5 points each · 10, J, Q, K = 10 points each · Aces = 15 points (played above the king, in a set, or caught in your hand) but only 5 points when played low, as the 1 in A-2-3.
  7. Jokers are WILD 🃏: two per deck. A joker can stand in for any card in a meld (one joker per meld). A melded joker is worth +50 points — but get caught holding one when the round ends and it's −50. High risk, high reward, very Wall Street.
  8. Joker heist: a melded joker shows which card it's standing in for. If you're holding that exact card, click the joker on your turn (after drawing, nothing selected) to swap your real card in and take the joker for yourself. You can even discard a joker — but the other player can scoop it right up.
  9. Round ends when someone plays their last card or the deck runs out. Everyone scores: melded points minus what's left in hand (yes, you can go negative — ouch 📉).
  10. Side bets 🎲 (online games only): tap the 🎲 button to bet chores, dinner, or bragging rights on the current round. Your opponent approves or denies it — if it's on, the round winner collects. Every settled bet is written down in the 📜 Bet book. No welching.
  11. Bull market: first player to 500 wins the game. Wins, losses, and bet records live under 📊 Stats on the menu.
